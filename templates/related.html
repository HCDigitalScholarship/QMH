The Framing Research

Abigal Corcoran '16, spent the summer of 2015 pouring through the materials available in Haverford Special Collections about Friends Asylum. The end product of her summer research was not only a framing essay titled 'A Mild and Appropriate System of Treatment': Moral Treatment and the Curability of Mental Illness at Friends Asylum on the early years of Friends Asylum and how the founders' Quakerism affected the way they dealt with mental health, but also the main informational content of this website.

The Scattergoood Letters

This online exhibit, curated by Jordan Nieusma '14 in the Summer of 2014, is made up of important and representative passages from the journals, letters, and memoirs of Thomas Scattergood (1748-1814). The website contains entries from Scattergood's journals which are unique in their content and the sentiments they convey, but overall convey the identity of a man lost in his spiritual uncertainty and accepting the guidance of his Inner Light.

Thomas Scattergood was a traveling minister, and he spent six years (1794-1800) visiting Quakers in England as a part of his ministry. He suffered from depression, and he was impressed by the Retreatâ€™s humane treatment of the mentally ill. When he returned to the United States, he argued that American Quakers should start an institution similar to the York Retreat. As such, after visiting the York Retreat, Thomas Scattergood was inspired and prompted other Quakers to think of creating what would eventually become Friends Asylum.
